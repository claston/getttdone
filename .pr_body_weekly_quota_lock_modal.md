## Summary

- Enforced a 7-day rolling quota window for anonymous conversions and kept `429` quota responses structured (`weekly_quota_exceeded`) with `reset_at` metadata.
- Reworked the quota-limit UX on `ofx-convert`: full-screen lock modal, simplified to two CTAs (`Create account` + `I already have an account`), and removed technical IDs (`analysis_id`, `processing_id`) from the download panel.
- Hardened auth/session transitions across `login`, `signup`, `client-area`, and `ofx-convert` to avoid stale-token loops and ensure explicit anonymous mode after logout.
- Made anonymous quota behavior explicit via env vars (`ANONYMOUS_QUOTA_LIMIT`, optional `UNLIMITED_ANON_QUOTA=true`) by removing the implicit dev-only `9999` fallback.

## Why

The free-conversion gating flow was inconsistent for launch validation: quota behavior was not weekly, the lock UI could get stuck after signup/login transitions, and anonymous-vs-authenticated quota boundaries were confusing during logout/testing. This PR makes quota rules deterministic and the conversion funnel behavior reliable for production rollout checks.
